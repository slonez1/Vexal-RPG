# Narrative Directions

## Purpose
This file contains GM prompts, story guidance, and narrative pacing notes to help shape the player's journey and maintain consistent storytelling.

## Current Narrative State
- **Current Arc**: Early Adventure - The player is establishing themselves in the world.
- **Tone**: Epic Fantasy with moments of introspection and moral complexity.
- **Pacing**: Moderate - Allow time for exploration and character development.

## Narrative Prompts

### Exploration Triggers
- When the player enters a new location, describe the atmosphere, NPCs present, and potential hooks.
- Offer 2-3 meaningful choices that advance the story or reveal world lore.

### Combat Encounters
- Before combat: Describe the enemy's appearance, motivations, and the stakes of the fight.
- During combat: Use environmental details to enhance immersion.
- After combat: Provide loot, XP, and narrative consequences.

### Character Interactions
- NPCs should have clear motivations and personality quirks.
- Use dialogue to reveal world lore, foreshadow events, or offer quests.
- Track NPC relationships and how player choices affect them.

### Moral Dilemmas
- Present choices where the "right" answer is unclear.
- Show consequences of player decisions in future encounters.
- Example: "A village elder asks you to retrieve a cursed artifact. Do you comply, refuse, or seek another solution?"

## Story Pacing Guidelines
- **Act 1**: Establish the world, introduce key NPCs, and set up the main conflict.
- **Act 2**: Escalate challenges, reveal deeper lore, and complicate the player's goals.
- **Act 3**: Bring plot threads together, force major decisions, and build toward climax.

## Foreshadowing Notes
- The Vexal condition hints at a larger cosmic threat.
- Divine Favor suggests Amara has caught the attention of celestial beings.
- The Blood Cult and Shadow Warden are connected—reveal this gradually.
